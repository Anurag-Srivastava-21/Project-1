Pandemics

Added: 08/06/20

An infectious disease that spreads across international boundaries, affecting a large number of people. For example, the most lethal pandemic recorded was The Plague(Black Death), killing up to 200 million people worldwide in the 14th century. Talking about the epidemic, it is also an infectious disease that rapidly affects a large number of people in a community in a short period. For instance, the 2014 West Africa Ebola epidemic. Pandemic is an epidemic that has spread over much larger area with more severity. Talking about the COVID-19, a disease caused by Coronavirus SARS-CoV2 is also declared a Pandemic lately by WHO (World Health Organization) Below, we have discussed the timeline of the past four pandemics that ruin the human population as well as the nation's economy.

Russian flu (1889-1890)

Source: google

This life-threatening influenza lasted from October 1889 to December 1990. It was first recorded in Bukhara, Russian Empire, in May 1889. By the end of the following year, it covered Northern America and Africa. The reason behind the rapid spread was the modern transport infrastructure. The ravaging flu took the lives of about 1 million people across the globe. Read more

Symptoms

Hot and cold flashes, along with body aches and fever were few major symptoms observed in the patients.

Spanish flu(1918-1919)

Source: google

The devastating flu with high mortality caused by H1N1 virus,infected 500 million people worldwide. 50 million deaths estimated, targeting young people with 20-40yrs of age group. The first case was witnessed at a military base in Kansas on 11th of March 1918, in US. Read more

Symptoms

Common flu symptoms like fatigue and fever were seen in the people infected by the virus.

Asian Flu(1957-1958)

Source: google

At the starting of 1957, a new influenza A (H2N2) virus outbroke in East Asia. It emerged from Guizhou, China. This havoc killed approx 1.5-2 million people. Wild ducks were the vectors of this flu. Read more

Symptoms

The symptoms were identical to the other flu, including fever, body aches, chills, cough, weakness, and loss of appetite.

SARS(2002-2003)

Source: google

The Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome was the first pandemic of the 21st Century, it was first spotted in Foshan, China. Nearly 8000 people were infected by SARS resulting in the loss of approx 700 lives. It is a deadly respiratory illness caused by coronavirus. Read more

Symptoms

Fever, headache, dry cough, and laboured breathing are some of the symptoms noticed in SARS patients.

Covid-19

Source: google

Coronaviruses are a large group of viruses containing single stranded RNA and are known to infect birds and mammals. 7 Coronaviruses can cause infection in human and out of them three can prove fatal. These are-

SARS-CoV2(Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome-Coronavirus 2) is a novel (new & different type) Coronavirus recognised as the cause of coronavirus disease 2019 that is COVID-19 that began in Wuhan city in China in late 2019 and thereafter spread worldwide.
MERS-CoV was the cause of Middle East Respiratory Syndrome in 2012.
SARS-CoV was recognised in 2003 and was the cause of severe acute respiratory syndrome in China at the end of 2002.
Coronavirus cause acute, mild upper respiratory infection (common cold). It invades the respiratory tract via nose. Transmission is via airborne droplets. Spread occuring from person to person via contact with respiratory droplets and nasal secretions. Patiens with symptoms (Symptomatic), without symptoms (Asymptomatic) and prior to symptoms (Presymptomatic) are known to cause infection. The incubation period ranges from 2 to 14 days after exposure to virus. The risk of serious disease and death due to COVID-19 is more in immunocompromised patients and in people with lung, liver, heart and kidney diseases. Aged, obese and diabetic people have high risk of getting infected. At initial stage, the person suffers from common cold, sore-throat, fever and weakness. The severity of disease is characterised by laboured breathing, hypoxia and extensive lung involvement which progresses to respiratory failure requiring mechanical ventilators. In addition to respiratory disease, it could progress to shock, multiorgan failure and ultimately death.

The outbreak of COVID-19 in late December 2019 started in Wuhan, China and was linked to an animal market which suggested its transmission from animal(bats) to human. However, its origin is still controversial. Since then it has spread worldwide. The outbreak of COVID-19 is monitored by Centers for Disease Control & Prevention(CDC). Earlier it was called an epidemic but its worldwide invasion has led WHO (World Health Organisation) to declare it as a Pandemic.

Symptoms

Fever, cough, cold, sore-throat, swollen adenoids, chills , muscle pain, weakness, laboured breathing or shortness of breath, pneumonia, loss of taste & smell and headache. Diarrhoea, vomiting or nausea may occur. Gastrointestinal problems are rare but may be seen. The illness is more severe in people above 65years of age. People with medical conditions like chronic lung disease, liver disease, serious heart disease, diabetes, obesity and immunocompromised patients. People involved in medical facilities like nurses, doctors and staff are more prone to infection.

Safety Measures

Transmission occurs through airborne droplets and nasal secretions therefore masks covering mouth and nose is a mandatory prevention measure. Hand sanitizers and soaps are used to get rid of infectious agents. Nationwide lockdown is followed for social distancing . A safe distance will prevent the spread of the virus. Basic measures include-

  • Stay home to stay safe.
  • Maintain social distance.
  • Wash hands and sanitize things if necessary.
  • Cover mouth while coughing and sneezing.
  • If feeling unwell, get a health checkup. Its a moral duty to not get others infected by oneself.
  • Go for self quarantine, if necessary.
  • Avoid unnecessary visits to any place.
  • Have eatable which boosts immunity.
  • Avoid touching of eyes, nose or mouth before washing hands.
