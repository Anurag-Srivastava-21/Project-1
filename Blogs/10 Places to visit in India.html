10 SPECTACULAR PLACES TO VISIT IN INDIA

Added: 10/07/20

Travel is something we all love to do. Exploring new places is always fascinating. Some people do it for fun, some to find peace and some other to revive from the monotony of life. The Art of Nature is feast for the eyes. The feel of travelling is beyond compare and the enthusiasm doubles when we have incredible destination. To name a few, we have

1. TUNGANATH, UTTARAKHAND-

Source: google

Tunganath means lord of the peaks, is one of the highest Shiva temples in the world and is located in Uttarakhand. It is located at an altitude of 3,680m and its summit is Chandrashilla which is just above Tunganath. Tunganath is one of the five Kedar temples. Chandrashilla is located at a height of about 4000m above sea level. From Chandrashilla, all you see are clouds everywhere, as far as your eyes can see. Standing over clouds feels like heaven. Incredible indeed!

2. NOHKALIKAI FALLS, MEGHALAYA

Source: google

Nohkalikai falls is the tallest plunge waterfall with a height of about 1115 feet and is located near Cherrapunji in Meghalaya. The view is very fascinating and adventurous. It feels like nature has made its own serene space with a touch of formidability. White water embraced with greenery all around is adorable.

3. DZUKOU VALLEY, NAGALAND

Source: google

Dzukou Valley is located at a height of 2452m above sea level in borders of Nagaland and Manipur in the eastern most part of North India. The valley is famous for its diverse flora and fauna, but it is well known for the Dzuko Lilly which is found only in this valley. One can get amazing trekking experience and adorable views. Greenery widespread and clouds floating around mountains. Such an amazing and serene place to find oneself.

4. LEH, LADAKH

Source: google

Leh is located at an altitude of 3524 m above sea level and is connected to Manali via Leh Manali highway, it is the road one must take to enjoy the beautiful mountains and amazing weather. The journey itself gives a heavenly feel. There are many places like Shanti Stupa, Gurudwara and the places around like Nubra Valley, Pangong lake which are must watch. The rocky snow-covered mountains all around, are to die for. Scenic view of surreal mountains is incredible. One of the perfect examples of nature's beauty.

6. MUNNAR, KERALA

Source: google

Munnar is a town in Western Ghats range of Kerala. It has numerous tourist places to offer like lush green forests, waterfalls, rivers, wildlife conservation, beautiful mountains, tea plantation estate, and much more. Its soothing weather all year round adds to its astounding beauty. Some of the tourist places include Kolukkumalai tea estate, Eravikulam National park - home for the Nilgiri Tahr, Kundala lake- offers a mesmerising view, Lakkom Water Fall- picturesque fall, Mattupetty Dam - sight -seeing and adventure spot, top station, Chinnar wildlife Sanctuary, and many more.

7. CHAND BAORI, RAJASTHAN

Source: google

Chand Baori is a massive stepwell located at Abhaneri Village of Rajasthan. Exploring Rajasthan which shelters various cultural heritage sites and has deeply rooted history is itself ravishing. Chand Baori is one of them. It was built by king Chanda somewhere around 9th Century. 33 crore idols of Gods and Goddesses are sculpted all around in the Baori. Folktales are suggestive of the fact that this gigantic unparalleled architecture having 3500 steps and 13 stories was built in just one night! It is believed to be a haunted place but beautifully crafted. It has a temple attached to it called Harshat Mata Mandir. Chand Baori was built to store water for use in times of crisis.

8. NANDA DEVI, UTTARAKHAND

Source: google

Nanda Devi is the second highest mountain in India after Kangchenjunga and 23rd highest peak in the world. Its name means bliss-giving Goddess and truly what a beautiful impregnable mountain. This lofty mountain offers places not only for fragile ecosystem but also has religious significance. Nanda Devi National Park which somewhat surrounds the mountains was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

9. MYSORE, KARNATAKA

Source: google

Mysore a city in Karnataka is native of various astonishing tourist places. In its centre lies the opulent Mysore Palace which is a blend of Hindu, Islamic, Gothic and Rajput style of architecture. Moreover, there is Brindavan garden, Chamundi hills, Jagamohan Palace - beautifully painted and having a good collection of clocks, St. Philomena Cathedral, Srirangapatna, Karanji lake- largest enclosure for birds, Krishna Raja Sagara Dam- hotspot tourist place, Lalitha mahal, Shivanasamundra falls, and much more.

10. AJANTA AND ELLORA CAVES

Source: google

Ajanta and Ellora Caves are located in Aurangabad district of Maharashtra. These are one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Ajanta Caves are rock cut Buddhist Cave monuments which presents the finest example of ancient Indian Art. One can find pictorial tales painted on the walls depicting life history of Buddha and Buddhist traditions. It has worship halls, sculpture of Buddhist deities and enchanting wall paintings. Ellora Caves is one of the largest monolithic rock-cut monasteries in the world. Cave 16 features a rock excavation, the Kailash Temple which is a chariot shaped monument dedicated to lord Shiva. There are over 100 caves having beautifully crafted sculptures and paintings.

These are some of the finest places that one must visit. India is a treasure trove of various mountains, valleys, forests, rivers, cultural sites, unparalleled architecture and what not. You must explore the rich diversity of India. Fall in love with India before you finally bid ‘Adieu’ to this world.
