Anxiety and depression

Added: 18/06/20

These two words have become very common now a days but how much we actually know about them? Let us know,

Anxiety

Anxiety is fear or worry about any situation or condition, past, present or future. It is very basic thing that people do get anxious and anticipate. It is quite a normal behaviour about what will happen or how it will happen. People get anxious when they have to take exam, go on a roller-coaster ride or may be an interview. While being anxious is normal, it keeps you focused and alert but when it stands in the way of your normal functioning of mind and body or when it interferes with your personal and professional life, you probably cross the dividing line of anxiety and anxiety disorder. Anxiety disorder evokes extreme fear or worry which is out of the proportion to the situation. You might refrain yourself from doing few things or getting in some situation owing to the fact that you might get extremely anxious and what if you have no body to look after you and what if the situation gets out of the control. In such cases, it is important to know that you are not alone. Few people try to hide their situation in view of their embarrassment. You must acquaint yourself with the fact that these problems need consideration and medical help otherwise this could make one mentally ill.

Symptoms of anxiety disorders

  • Surge of overwhelming fear.
  • Feeling of losing control from anything to everything.
  • Pounding heart.
  • Feeling of blackout.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Sweating
  • Trembling or shaking.
  • Feeling detached.
  • Hot and Cold flashes and chills.
  • Upset Stomach.
  • Restlessness.
  • Insomnia.

Panic attack, OCD, etc are part of anxiety disorder. Hormonal imbalance also causes panic attacks. Adrenaline and cortisol are stress hormone which help to combat with stress. Apart from it, thyroid imbalance influence anxiety. When excessive thyroid hormone is released, it increases heartbeat, shakiness and excessive sweating occurs. Hormonal imbalance leads to variance in normal metabolism of body.

Depression

Second common yet major problem is depression. Depression is the state of feeling low, feeling of failure, worthlessness, loneliness and hopelessness. When we lose something, or do not get success somewhere or all our efforts go in vain, it causes depression. Slight depression is common as people do face ups and downs in life but when this depression becomes persistent and cause significant impairment in daily life, it becomes a disorder. Possible cause includes loss of loved ones, poverty, physical illness, failure and might be some unpleasant life events.

Symptoms of Depression

  • Sadness.
  • Might also feel anxious.
  • Loss of interest in any or every kind of activity.
  • Feeling of worthlessness.
  • Losing concentration and dedication.
  • Hopelessness.
  • Pessimistic thinking or beliefs.
  • Irritability.
  • Restlessness.
  • Insomnia.
  • Feeling Fatigue.
  • Suicidal thoughts.
  • Gastrointestinal problems.
  • Ignorant Conduct.

Neurotransmitter as serotonin, dopamine or norepinephrine affect emotions and in people with depression, they may be out of balance. Antidepressants work to balance them.

Ways to cope with anxiety and depression includes

  • Connect with people and try to open up. Loneliness triggers the problems of anxiety and depression. Once you talk about your problems, they seem less overwhelming.
  • Practice meditation. Calm mind can rejuvenate the body and life. It helps to deal with restless mental state.


    • “Meditation and concentration are the ways to a life of serenity”

    Ram Das.



  • Get enough sleep. Lack of sleep makes one irritable and effects daily routine. Irritable mind creates anxious and depressive thoughts.
  • Try to stay happy and put an end to chronic worrying.
  • Try leading a healthy life by eating healthy and through regular exercise.

Anxiety and depression are common yet a major deterrent. Prevalence of these emotions can be seen in almost every age group but these can be dealt with. Consideration, socialism and medication can be of great help.
